Phishing

Malicious software that uses deception and manipulation to fool users into disclosing sensitive information like login passwords, financial information, or personal information is known as "phishing malware." It often operates through phoney emails, texts, or websites that impersonate trustworthy organisations like banks, social media sites, or online stores. Cybercriminals use the information they obtain from victims after tricking them into giving it to them in order to steal identities or make money. Phishing malware relies on social engineering strategies and frequently uses urgency or fear tactics to persuade users to take immediate action without first confirming the legitimacy of the request. Users should use caution when responding with unwanted emails or messages, confirm the legitimacy of websites, and use dependable security software to identify and prevent phishing virus.

Example

John receives an email seemingly from his bank, urgently requesting him to update his account information due to a security breach. The email contains a link that directs him to a fake website designed to mimic the bank's official site, tricking him into entering his sensitive login credentials. Unaware of the scam, John falls victim to phishing and inadvertently compromises his personal banking information.
